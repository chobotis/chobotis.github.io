Brain Tumor Detection and Classification in MRI Scans

The brain is a continuous enigma.

How do we examine a still image of it to determine whether or not there is a tumor inside?

Datasets

. Employing Python libraries, Cancer Imaging Archives datasets, DICOM, and advanced deep learning methodologies, I developed and deployed two proficient deep learning models for precisely detecting brain tumors in MRI scans.

The models

The two models, a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) and a Support Vector Machine (SVM), underwent comprehensive training and testing with diverse variants to achieve optimal performance.

Computer Vision in Action

By operationalizing cutting-edge computer vision and machine-learning techniques, a robust neural network was created, exhibiting exceptional proficiency in accurately identifying anomalies within a dataset comprising 1000 images.

Accuracy Rates

The Neural Network achieved an impressive average accuracy rate of 95% to 99%.
